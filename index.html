Adventures in Fire Island

Fire Island
National Seashore

A week of salt air and silent dunes. Waking up to the sound of waves breaking along the barrier island was pure magic. We hiked the Sunken Forest trail — a maritime holly forest unlike anything else — and climbed the historic Fire Island Lighthouse at sunrise. The wildlife was stunning: egrets, foxes, and even a glimpse of a seal offshore. Kayaking through the Great South Bay and staying in the car-free communities of Cherry Grove and Ocean Beach felt like stepping back in time. The starry nights above the wilderness area reminded me how wild Long Island’s coast still is. A true hidden gem of the National Park system.
